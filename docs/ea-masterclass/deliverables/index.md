# Deliverables Overview

This section contains all 20 deliverable templates produced across the Enterprise Architect Masterclass. Each template is designed to be copy-paste ready — a practical starting point that you adapt to your organisation and engagement. The templates are organised into three groups reflecting the primary contexts in which enterprise architects produce work: building business cases, operating within governance structures, and leading consulting engagements.

!!! info "How to Use These Templates"
    These templates are not documents to fill in mechanically. They are structured starting points. Before using any template, read the relevant module content to understand the thinking behind it. A template without context produces a document; a template used with judgment produces a recommendation.

---

## Group 1: Business Case Templates (Templates 1–6)

[Go to Business Case Templates →](business-case-templates.md)

These six templates support the end-to-end process of designing, building, and presenting an enterprise AI investment case. They range from structural guides (how to organise an EA handbook) through to complete, ready-to-use document templates.

**Template 1 — Enterprise Architecture Handbook (Structure)**

A structured table of contents and chapter guide for an enterprise architecture handbook. Use this when establishing or refreshing the EA function in an organisation. It defines what belongs in a canonical EA handbook, what the purpose of each chapter is, and what a reader should be able to do after reading it. The handbook structure covers strategy, principles, methods, domain architectures, governance, and tooling — and is designed to be adopted in whole or progressively built out chapter by chapter.

**Template 2 — Enterprise Proposal Playbook**

A step-by-step operational playbook for producing a technology proposal in an enterprise context. Rather than a document template, this is a process guide: it defines every stage from initial brief through to stakeholder approval, with key activities, participants, time estimates, and outputs at each stage. Use it to manage proposal development as a disciplined process, not an ad-hoc writing exercise.

**Template 3 — AI Technology Investment Guide**

A structured evaluation guide for technology investment decisions in the AI domain. This template provides evaluation criteria, a vendor scoring matrix, a build-versus-buy decision framework, and a set of due diligence questions for AI vendors. It is designed to be used during the discovery and evaluation phase of an AI investment — before the business case is written, while options are still being assessed.

**Template 4 — Executive Business Case Template**

The complete 11-section business case template from Module 6, presented here as a clean, standalone document. All sections are included with placeholder text and guidance notes explaining what content belongs in each section, what length is appropriate, and what executives look for when reviewing it. This is the most-used template in the collection — it applies to virtually every significant technology investment proposal.

**Template 5 — Proposal Writing Handbook**

A practical guide to the craft of proposal writing, covering the full lifecycle from pre-writing discovery through to final submission. It includes a structured approach to each writing phase, common mistakes and how to avoid them, and a 25-point quality checklist for use before submitting any proposal. Use it as a reference guide during proposal development and as a review tool before submission.

**Template 6 — Enterprise AI Strategy Playbook**

A structured playbook for developing an enterprise AI strategy from scratch. It walks through six stages: defining the AI vision, assessing current maturity, identifying and prioritising use cases, designing the operating model, planning the technology platform, and establishing governance. Each stage includes the key questions to answer, the outputs to produce, and the stakeholders to involve. Use it when leading an AI strategy engagement or when the organisation has asked "where do we start with AI?"

## Group 2: Governance Templates (Templates 7–10)

[Go to Governance Templates →](governance-templates.md)

These four templates support the governance dimension of enterprise architecture: making investment decisions rigorously, managing architecture review, governing AI responsibly, and building financial models that executives can interrogate.

**Template 7 — Technology Investment Decision Framework**

A scoring model for evaluating and prioritising technology investments against a consistent set of criteria. It defines five evaluation dimensions (strategic fit, financial return, risk, feasibility, and urgency), provides weighting guidance, and includes a scoring rubric and aggregation formula. Use it when an organisation has multiple competing investment proposals and needs a structured, defensible basis for prioritisation.

**Template 8 — Architecture Review Board Guide**

A complete operations guide for establishing and running an Architecture Review Board. It includes a charter template, membership role definitions, the decision-making process, submission requirements, a sample ARB agenda, and a decision record format. Use it when setting up an ARB from scratch or when refreshing an existing ARB that has become ineffective. It is also useful as a reference when preparing an ARB submission.

**Template 9 — AI Governance Playbook**

A governance framework specifically designed for enterprise AI programmes. It covers responsible AI principles, the use case review process, a model risk management checklist, data ethics review requirements, and ongoing monitoring standards. Use it when an organisation is deploying AI at scale and needs to move beyond ad-hoc oversight to a structured, auditable governance process that satisfies both internal risk standards and external regulatory requirements.

**Template 10 — Financial Modeling Guide for Architects**

A practical guide to building the financial models that support investment decisions, with blank templates included for immediate use. It covers the five-year total cost of ownership (TCO) model, NPV and IRR calculations with example formulas, sensitivity analysis, and scenario analysis. It is designed for architects who need to produce financial models without a dedicated finance team — it explains the concepts, provides the structure, and gives enough worked examples to produce a credible model independently.

## Group 3: Consulting Templates (Templates 11–20)

[Go to Consulting Templates →](consulting-templates.md)

These ten templates support the consulting dimension of enterprise architecture: structuring engagements, asking the right questions, presenting to executives, reviewing work for quality, planning AI transformations, producing standard architecture documents, and building self-assessment and learning tools.

**Template 11 — Consulting Workshop Toolkit**

A complete guide to designing and facilitating architecture and strategy workshops, including agenda templates for discovery workshops, facilitation techniques for different group dynamics, virtual workshop considerations, and output formats. Use it when preparing for any workshop where the goal is to extract insights from a group of stakeholders — whether a 2-hour discovery session or a 2-day strategy sprint.

**Template 12 — Discovery Questionnaire Library**

A library of 50 structured discovery questions organised across five categories: business strategy, technology landscape, data and AI readiness, governance maturity, and organisational readiness. Each question is designed to surface insights that are rarely volunteered in unstructured conversations. Use this library when preparing for stakeholder interviews — selecting and adapting the most relevant questions for each audience.

**Template 13 — Executive Presentation Templates**

Three complete presentation outlines — each including slide title, content guidance, and design notes — for the three most common executive presentation contexts: an investment approval pitch (10 slides), a board AI readiness update (6 slides), and an Architecture Review Board presentation (8 slides). Use these as starting structures, adapting content to the specific organisation and opportunity.

**Template 14 — Proposal Review Checklists**

Three distinct checklists for the proposal review process: a 25-item self-review checklist for the author to use before sharing the draft, a 15-item peer review checklist for a colleague reviewing the draft, and a 10-item final pre-submission checklist. Using all three in sequence significantly reduces the probability of a proposal being rejected for avoidable reasons.

**Template 15 — AI Transformation Roadmap Template**

A structured format for building a 3-year AI transformation roadmap. It defines the x-axis (quarters), the y-axis (workstreams: Data Foundation, Platform, Use Cases, Governance, Organisation), milestone types, and dependency notation conventions. Use it to turn an AI strategy into a sequenced delivery plan that executives can track and fund in phases.

**Template 16 — Enterprise Architecture Deliverable Templates**

Standard templates for the four most common architecture documents: current-state architecture document, target-state architecture document, architecture decision record (ADR), and architecture principle statement. These are the building blocks of an EA practice's document library — standardising them saves time, improves consistency, and makes architecture review faster.

**Template 17 — Enterprise AI Case Study Workbook**

A blank workbook for use alongside the Module 13 case studies. It provides structured prompts for each of the 12 engagement phases — from initial scoping through to value realisation review. Use it when working through the case studies to turn observation into practice, or use it on real engagements as a running engagement journal.

**Template 18 — Enterprise Architect Competency Matrix (Self-Assessment Tool)**

A clean, self-assessment version of the competency matrix from Module 14. It is formatted with rating scales, evidence prompts, and space for mentor commentary. Use it at the start and end of the 90-day practice plan, and repeat it annually as a career development tool.

**Template 19 — 90-Day Enterprise Architect Practice Plan (Workbook)**

A workbook version of the 90-day plan from Module 14, with structured spaces for recording: key stakeholders and their priorities, governance bodies attended and what was learned, the topic of the first proposal, the name and contact of the assigned mentor, and reflection notes at each checkpoint. Use it to make the 90-day plan a live document rather than a static checklist.

**Template 20 — Capstone Project Brief**

The complete Capstone Challenge from Module 14, formatted as a standalone project brief. It includes the full specification for all eight parts, evaluation criteria for each part, a submission checklist, and a self-assessment rubric. Use it to run the Capstone as a formal learning programme, or use it as a structured portfolio project to demonstrate EA capability to a prospective employer or client.
