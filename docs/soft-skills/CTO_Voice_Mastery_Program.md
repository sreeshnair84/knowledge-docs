## Week 1 of 12 — Your Voice is an Instrument

### Day 1 — The Habitual Voice — Breaking Free  *[VINH FRAMEWORK]*

Good morning. I'm glad you showed up today. That already puts you ahead of 90% of people who say they want to change. Here is the most important thing I will ever tell you: the voice you have right now — the quiet, trailing, soft-spoken architect voice — that is NOT who you are. That is who you practiced being for 20 years. And anything that was practiced can be un-practiced. We start today.

Understand that your communication patterns are habits, not identity. Your job is not to change who you are — it is to stop limiting who you can become.

Vinh Giang's foundational concept: Your voice is not your natural voice — it is your habitual voice. A series of behaviors repeated so long they feel fixed. The moment you understand this, everything becomes possible.

Record 3 minutes of yourself talking about any project you know well. No preparation. Just talk. Save the file. Label it 'Week 1 Day 1 Baseline.' Do not judge it yet. We will return to it on Day 84. This is your before photo.

Ask yourself: In what specific situations today did my habitual voice kick in? When did I trail off? When did I go quiet? Write down 2 examples.

📚 Source: Vinh Giang, vinhgiang.com: 'The voice you have now is actually your habitual voice — just a series of habits you have been repeating for so long that you have come to believe it is your natural sound.'

### Day 2 — Rate of Speech — The Speed of Passion  *[VINH FRAMEWORK]*

Welcome back. Yesterday you recorded your baseline. Today we start building. I want you to think about a great speaker you admire — maybe a TED speaker, a CEO you have seen present. Notice how they never stay at one speed. They surge and pull back. They race through setup and slow way down for the landing. That contrast is not accidental. It is the single most neglected tool in your kit. Today we fix that.

Consciously vary your rate of speech within every answer, every explanation, every call. Use speed for context, use slowness for conclusions.

Vocal Foundation #1: Rate of Speech. Speed signals emotion. Fast speech = passion and excitement. Slow speech = weight and profundity. A flat, unchanging rate is the fastest way to lose an audience — even if your content is brilliant.

Open any book. Read one paragraph at your normal pace. Now re-read it: rush through the scene-setting, then dramatically slow down the final key sentence. Do this 10 times with 10 different paragraphs. Time yourself: 4 minutes. Feel the difference.

On your next call today, catch yourself speaking at a flat rate. Intentionally slow your last sentence of any key point by 50%. Count how many times you do this successfully.

📚 Source: Vinh Giang, UNSTOPPABLE Podcast Transcript (ytscribe.com): 'When I go quickly it shows passion, but when I slow down — I am saying nothing important here — but it seems so profound. Rate of speech is its own beast.'

### Day 3 — Volume — The Sound of Authority  *[VINH FRAMEWORK]*

Let me tell you something I tell every CEO I coach. I was working with a CEO of a major fitness brand — he spoke at a level 3 volume. When I coached him to level 5 he said to me: Vinh, I think I am being too loud. I said: Dave, you are losing authority, charisma, and confidence every single day by staying at level 3. You are a CEO. Your voice should fill the room, not hide in the corner of it. The same goes for you. Twenty years of architecture knowledge sitting behind a voice that does not carry it. That ends today.

Commit to speaking at Level 6 in every professional interaction today. It will feel wrong. Good. That wrongness is progress.

Vocal Foundation #2: Volume. Volume is the fastest signal of confidence. Speaking at Level 3 (soft) signals that you yourself are not sure your words matter. Speaking at Level 6-7 signals absolute conviction. The great irony: when told to raise their volume, most soft-spoken professionals feel they are 'being too loud.' That feeling is your calibration being wrong — not your volume.

Chest resonance exercise: Place your hand on your chest. Hum 'mmmmm' until you feel vibration. Now speak from that same place: 'My recommendation for this architecture is...' Project from the chest, not the throat. Do this for 5 minutes before any important call.

How many times today did you catch yourself dropping to Level 3? What triggered it — a specific person, a type of question, feeling put on the spot?

📚 Source: Vinh Giang, UNSTOPPABLE Podcast & 5 Vocal Foundations Video (vinhgiang.com): 'Volume shows confidence, authority, that you believe in what you are saying. People who speak too quietly are losing out on a huge amount of authority, charisma, and energy.'

### Day 4 — Julian Treasure — The 7 Deadly Sins  *[JULIAN FRAMEWORK]*

I want to bring in a mentor of mine today — Julian Treasure. Five-time TED speaker, sound expert. Over 100 million views on his talks. Julian and I agree on this: before you learn to speak powerfully, you must learn to stop speaking destructively. You are 20 years into a career where you have strong, well-earned opinions. But opinion delivered without openness sounds like dogmatism — and dogmatism is the single biggest reason smart people stop being listened to. Today we audit your sins.

Identify which of the 7 sins you are most guilty of in professional settings. Most architects unconsciously default to dogmatism or negativity. Own it so you can fix it.

Julian Treasure's TED Talk framework: Before we can speak powerfully, we must stop speaking destructively. The 7 Deadly Sins of Speaking are habits that cause people to tune out — gossip, judging, negativity, complaining, excuses, embellishment, and dogmatism. In corporate tech settings, the most common culprit for senior architects is dogmatism: speaking as if your technical view is the only valid view.

In your next two meetings, count silently every time you feel the urge to be dismissive of an alternative view. Do not suppress the thought — just notice it. For each one, force yourself to say: 'Tell me more about how you arrived at that.' Practice the pause before the opinion.

Which of the 7 sins did you catch yourself falling into today? Write down the specific sentence or moment. What would the HAIL version of that moment sound like?

📚 Source: Julian Treasure, TED Global 2013, 'How to Speak So That People Want to Listen' (ted.com, 100M+ views): 'There are a number of habits we need to move away from. I have assembled 7 deadly sins of speaking — gossip, judging, negativity, complaining, excuses, embellishment, dogmatism.'

### Day 5 — The HAIL Framework — Your Communication Foundation  *[JULIAN FRAMEWORK]*

Here is your Friday framework. Julian Treasure calls these the cornerstones. HAIL. I love this word because it means to greet or acclaim with enthusiasm — which is exactly how people will respond to you when you stand on these four pillars. Honesty: say what is actually true, even when it is uncomfortable. Authenticity: do not try to sound like a different person — amplify the best of who you already are. Integrity: do what you said you would do, every time, in every meeting. Love: not romantic love — the desire for others to succeed. When you genuinely wish the people in your meeting well, something changes in how you sound. Try it and see.

Choose one HAIL pillar to consciously activate in every interaction today. Start with Authenticity — stop performing 'architect mode' and just speak as yourself, with your full knowledge.

Julian Treasure's HAIL Framework: Honesty, Authenticity, Integrity, Love. These are not soft concepts — they are the structural foundation that determines whether people trust you before you even finish your first sentence. For a CTO, HAIL is how you build the credibility that makes people follow your technical vision.

Before your first call today, write one sentence that is completely honest — something you have been softening or dancing around for weeks. Practice saying it out loud, clearly, at Level 6 volume, with no qualifiers. Use it in today's meeting.

Which HAIL pillar felt most foreign today? Where did you feel yourself slipping out of it? What situation triggered the lapse?

📚 Source: Julian Treasure, TED Global 2013 & Podwise summary (podwise.org): 'HAIL — Honesty: being true, straight, clear. Authenticity: being yourself, standing in your own truth. Integrity: being your word. Love: wishing people well — it is nearly impossible to judge someone while you are genuinely wishing them well.'

## Week 2 of 12 — The Full Vocal Toolbox

### Day 1 — Pitch & Melody — The 88-Key Piano  *[VINH FRAMEWORK]*

Happy Monday. New week. By now your volume is starting to shift — you may have had someone comment that you seem 'different' or more 'present.' Hold that. Today we go deeper into what makes a voice magnetic: melody. I want you to think of the most boring speaker you have ever sat through. What was their voice like? Flat. Same note. Same note. Same note. Now think of the most captivating speaker. Every sentence moved. High energy on key points, low and slow on important ones. They played the full piano. Today, we find your octaves.

Start noticing your own pitch range when you speak. You likely hover at one note. Today's mission is to deliberately go higher on words that carry excitement and lower on words that carry weight.

Vocal Foundation #3: Pitch. Most people use 2-3 notes their entire lives. Vinh's piano metaphor: you have 88 keys available — a full dynamic range from low authority tones to high energy enthusiasm. Melody is what makes words memorable. Music has melody; this is why you can remember a song lyric but not a page in a book.

The Siren Exercise: Hum from your lowest comfortable note to your highest, like a siren. Do this for 2 minutes. Then read one paragraph from any document — but this time sing-speak it: exaggerate the pitch changes like a storyteller telling a fairy tale to a child. It will feel absurd. Do it anyway. Your real-world pitch use will calibrate to about 30% of your practice intensity — so over-exaggerate now.

Pick one sentence you said today that you felt was delivered flatly. Write it down. Practice saying it with full pitch variation — record it, listen back.

📚 Source: Vinh Giang, UNSTOPPABLE Podcast Transcript (ytscribe.com): 'You have a piano of 88 keys and most people speak with a couple of keys. Music has melody — so if you speak with more melody, what you say becomes more memorable.'

### Day 2 — Tonality & Emotion — Your Face Drives Your Voice  *[VINH FRAMEWORK]*

I am going to give you a simple test today. I want you to say this sentence right now, wherever you are reading this: 'This architecture will reduce our latency by 40 percent.' Say it with a completely neutral face. How did it sound? Now smile slightly — just the corners of your eyes — and say it again. Different sound, yes? That is not magic. That is muscle. Your facial expression is upstream of your voice. Change the face, change everything.

Today is about face-voice alignment. Warm your face before you warm your voice. Before every call, hold a genuine smile for 5 seconds — not performance, but recall of something that actually makes you feel positive.

Vocal Foundation #4: Tonality. Your vocal emotion is directly generated by your facial expression. A flat face produces a flat voice. A warm face produces a warm voice. This is not performance — it is physiology. The muscles of your face directly affect your larynx and resonance chambers. Animate the face; transform the voice.

Record a 90-second explanation of any technical topic twice. Take 1: completely neutral face. Take 2: allow your face to genuinely express interest, curiosity, and enthusiasm as you speak. Listen to both back-to-back. Note the difference in vocal color, energy, and believability.

When today did you catch your face going flat? What was happening in the room or call at that moment? How did the people around you seem to respond?

📚 Source: Vinh Giang, Key Communication Lessons (oratoryclub.com): 'Tonality and your facial expressions are linked — aligning the two enhances authenticity and impact. Your facial expressions affect your vocal emotion.'

### Day 3 — The Power of the Pause  *[COMBINED MASTERS]*

Today we talk about nothing. Literally — the nothing between your words. The pause. Julian Treasure says silence is the foundation of all powerful speaking. I say the pause is where your authority lives. Every filler word — um, uh, so, basically, you know — is a panic response to silence. You are terrified of the gap, so you fill it with noise. But here is what the audience hears when you pause: conviction. Certainty. The sense that you chose every single word. That is the voice of a CTO. Today you learn to sit in the silence and own it.

Eliminate filler words by replacing them with intentional pauses. Every um becomes a breath. Every so becomes a moment of eye contact. Every basically becomes a pivot.

Both Vinh Giang and Julian Treasure independently identify the Pause as the most underused and most powerful tool in all of communication. The pause does three things: it gives the audience time to process, it signals that what was just said matters, and it projects authority. Amateurs fill silence with 'um.' Leaders sit in it.

The 1-10 Energy Drill (Vinh): Count from 1 to 10, with a full 2-second pause between each number. Do not rush. Do not fill. Just breathe and hold. Do 3 rounds. Then apply it to your next live conversation: when you feel an um coming, breathe instead. Count how many fillers you successfully replaced today.

How did the pause feel in real conversations? Did people wait for you? Were they more attentive? What was the internal discomfort level — and did it decrease by conversation 3?

📚 Source: Vinh Giang, Jay Shetty 'On Purpose' Podcast (iheart.com): 'The pause allows people to process what you are saying. The reason people are not comfortable with the pause is because they do not know what the pause is for.' / Julian Treasure, TED Talk transcript (singjupost.com): 'Silence is such a powerful tool — being comfortable with silence is the foundation of all powerful speaking.'

### Day 4 — Julian's Vocal Toolbox — Register, Timbre & Prosody  *[JULIAN FRAMEWORK]*

Julian Treasure has a concept I love: the vocal toolbox. Most people have never opened it. They speak from wherever the sound happens to come out, with no awareness of the levers available to them. Today I want you to find your chest register — the foundation of vocal authority. When you speak from your chest, you create resonance. When you speak from your throat or nose, you create effort. Leaders resonate. They do not strain. This single shift — from throat to chest — will make you sound 10 years more senior overnight.

Find your chest register and anchor every important sentence in it. This is especially critical for your opening sentence on any call — it sets the tonal frame for everything that follows.

Julian Treasure's Vocal Toolbox includes six practical levers: Register (where in your body the voice comes from), Timbre (the texture and warmth of your sound), Prosody (the musical quality of speech patterns), Pace (speed modulation), Pitch (high/low range), and Volume. Register is where Treasure diverges from most coaches — he teaches that speaking from the chest, not the throat or nose, creates the deepest authority.

Register Find: Place palm on chest. Hum low until you feel chest vibration. Sustain it for 10 seconds. Now speak from that same place: 'Good morning everyone, here is what we are resolving today.' Repeat 10 times. Record and listen. Then: avoid any upward inflection at sentence ends — do not make statements sound like questions. This is Julian's prosody fix for the 'uncertain voice.'

Did anyone react differently to your voice today? Did you feel the chest register shift in live conversation? Where did you struggle to maintain it?

📚 Source: Julian Treasure, 'How to Speak So That People Want to Listen' (singjupost.com): 'Register — speak from the chest for depth and authority. Timbre affects how people perceive you — rich and warm tones are far more appealing. Prosody: repetitive upward inflection makes statements sound like questions and restricts your ability to communicate through your voice.'

### Day 5 — The Vocal Warm-Up Ritual  *[JULIAN FRAMEWORK]*

Friday. And I want to give you something you will use for the rest of your life. A ritual. The pre-call ritual. Julian Treasure demonstrates this in his TED Talk — he calls it warming up the toolbox. I have modified it for our purposes and combined it with my own warm-up sequence. This takes 7 minutes. You do it before any important call, presentation, or executive meeting. It will transform how you sound from the first word. Not the fifth minute — the first word. Your first word sets everything. Warm up before it.

Install the 7-minute pre-call ritual as a non-negotiable habit. Do not walk into a meeting cold. Your voice needs preparation, just like your slides.

Julian Treasure's Pre-Speaking Warm-Up Protocol: Before any important speaking occasion, a physical warm-up of the voice, lips, and tongue dramatically improves clarity, resonance, and authority. This is not optional for elite communicators — it is standard. Athletes warm up their muscles. Communicators must warm up their instrument.

The 7-Minute Pre-Call Ritual — do this before your next important meeting: (1) 60 sec: Siren humming — low to high and back, 3 cycles. (2) 60 sec: Lip trills — blow air through closed lips making a motorboat sound. (3) 60 sec: Over-articulation — say 'red leather yellow leather' 10 times, exaggerating every consonant. (4) 60 sec: Volume ladder — say your opening sentence at Level 3, 5, 7, then settle at 6. (5) 60 sec: Pause practice — say your key point, then hold 3 seconds of silence. (6) 60 sec: Chest register anchor — hum, feel vibration, speak your first sentence from that place. (7) 60 sec: Face warm — smile, animate, think of something genuinely good.

Did the ritual change how the meeting started? Could you feel the difference in your voice? Which step had the most impact?

📚 Source: Julian Treasure, TED Global 2013 (singjupost.com): 'Finally — no toolbox works unless the engine is heated. Julian demonstrates 6 simple exercises which can be done to warm up the voice, lips, and tongue — this should be done before any important speaking occasion.' / Vinh Giang: 5 Vocal Foundations warm-up protocol (vinhgiang.com).

## Week 3 of 12 — The 4 Vocal Archetypes

### Day 1 — The 4 Vocal Archetypes — Who Are You When You Speak?  *[VINH FRAMEWORK]*

Good morning. Week 3. We are moving from your instrument to your identity. And I want to start with a question that might be uncomfortable: When you walk into a room or join a call, who are you? Not your job title — who are you as a communicator? Because right now, I will bet everything that you are always the Educator. Measured. Structured. Accurate. Valuable — but incomplete. Today we map your archetype profile and start building the full range. A CTO needs all four. Not in equal measure — but all four must be available when needed.

Identify your default archetype (almost certainly Educator) and understand what the other three feel like. Today is awareness only. Do not force a change yet — just notice which archetype you are in at each moment.

Vinh Giang's Vocal Archetypes: The Motivator, the Educator, the Coach, and the Friend. These are the four primary communication modes that great speakers switch between deliberately. Most people are stuck in one default archetype their entire career. Technical professionals almost universally default to the Educator — informing without connecting or inspiring.

Watch any 5-minute video of a leader you admire. Identify which archetype they are using in each section: Friend (warm, melodic opening), Motivator (fast, expansive, high energy), Educator (structured, measured), Coach (direct, assertive, no filler). Note how many times they switch. Now watch a video of yourself (your Week 1 baseline). Which archetypes appear? Which never appear?

In today's meetings, which archetypes did you observe in others? Which archetype were you? Was there a moment when the room needed a different archetype than the one you were in?

📚 Source: Vinh Giang, LinkedIn & Stage Academy (linkedin.com/in/vinhgiang): 'There are many vocal archetypes you use that you may be unaware of — the four most common are the Educator, the Friend, the Motivator, and the Coach. Dynamic and charismatic communicators master vocal archetypes and switch between them with ease.'

### Day 2 — The Friend — Connection First, Everything Else Second  *[VINH FRAMEWORK]*

Here is the data behind the Friend archetype. When you open a meeting as an Educator — 'So today we will cover three things: first...' — you have put people into receiving mode. They are waiting to be taught. But when you open as a Friend — 'Good to see you all. I have been thinking about this challenge all week and I genuinely think we are close to something good here' — you have activated belonging. People now want to think WITH you, not just listen TO you. That is the shift from architect to leader.

Today: open every single call or meeting as a Friend for the first 60 seconds, before switching to Educator mode. Warm, personal, genuine — not scripted.

The Friend Archetype: Warm, melodic, conversational, relaxed body language. This is the archetype that builds psychological safety, creates openness, and makes people lean toward you before you have shared a single piece of information. For technical leaders, the Friend is the most uncomfortable archetype — it feels 'soft.' It is, in fact, your most powerful opening move.

Write three 'Friend openers' for the three most common types of calls you run. Examples: Status update: 'Before we dive in — I genuinely appreciate how this team has been pushing through this sprint.' Architecture review: 'I want to start by saying this problem is hard and the fact that we are all in this room is a good sign.' Escalation call: 'I know this has been stressful for everyone — I have been there too. Let us solve it together.' Memorize them. Use them this week.

How did the Friend opener land? Did you notice any change in the energy of the room in the first 5 minutes? Did anyone seem more engaged or responsive?

📚 Source: Keefe Chee, Stage Academy Review (keefechee.com): 'The Friend connects — melodic tone, conversational rhythm, relaxed body language. The winning sequence: Connection first as Friend, then why as Motivator, then what as Educator, then how as Coach. Data without connection is sterile — the room may understand you, but they will not feel you.'

### Day 3 — The Motivator — Ignite the Why  *[VINH FRAMEWORK]*

I want you to think about the last time you got genuinely excited in a meeting. You probably felt it internally but kept it mostly contained — professional, measured. Here is what happened on the other side of that screen or table: the people with you missed it. They saw the Educator. They needed the Motivator — the version of you that says 'This matters. This changes things. Here is why we are all going to care about this deeply.' Today you learn to let that out. Controlled. Deliberate. But real.

Find the WHY of each technical topic you discuss today. Before any Educator segment, deliver one Motivator sentence: why this matters, what it changes, what is at risk if we do not solve it.

The Motivator Archetype: Higher volume, faster rate, expansive gestures, forward leaning energy. This archetype answers the most important unspoken question in any meeting: Why should I care? Without a Motivator moment early in a presentation or call, people show up physically but not mentally. For a CTO-level communicator, the Motivator is your opening statement of consequence.

The Stakes Statement: For your most important topic this week, write a 30-second Motivator opening using this structure: [Current problem] + [What breaks if we do not fix it] + [What becomes possible if we do]. Example: 'Right now our API response times are killing conversion on mobile. Every 100ms of latency costs us 1% in revenue. If we solve this by Q2 we unlock the entire Southeast Asia expansion.' Practice at Level 7 volume, faster than normal, leaning slightly forward. Record it. Own it.

Did you feel the shift when you switched into Motivator mode? How did the room respond? Was it uncomfortable to show that level of conviction and energy? Good — that discomfort is growth.

📚 Source: Keefe Chee, Stage Academy Review (keefechee.com) and Vinh Giang Stage Academy Module 3 (coursesdwn.com): 'The Motivator inspires — higher volume, faster rate, expansive gestures. Dynamic and charismatic communicators master vocal archetypes and switch between them with ease.'

### Day 4 — The Coach — Authority Without Aggression  *[VINH FRAMEWORK]*

The Coach archetype is the one most soft-spoken professionals fear. It feels aggressive. It feels like you are being rude or demanding. But I want you to flip that perception right now. The Coach is not aggressive — the Coach is clear. And in a world of ambiguous corporate communication, clarity is a gift. When you say 'I need this done by Thursday' instead of 'So if we could kind of wrap this up sometime this week that would be great,' you are not being harsh — you are respecting everyone's time by removing uncertainty. That is leadership.

Today: use the Coach voice for every commitment, deadline, and decision. No qualifiers. No trailing off. Clear ask. Clear timeline. Clear owner.

The Coach Archetype: Direct, assertive, almost no filler words, short punchy staccato delivery, strong body language with commanding gestures. The Coach does not explain — the Coach directs. For a CTO, the Coach voice is what you use when you need a decision made, an action committed to, or a problem owned. It is the voice of clarity.

The Coach Commitment Formula: Practice this structure for every action item from today's meetings: '[Name], I need [specific deliverable] by [specific date/time], so that [reason]. Can you commit to that?' The final question invites ownership without being demanding. Say it at Level 6, no filler, complete stop at the end. Do this in at least 2 real interactions today.

How did people respond to direct, clear requests? Did anyone seem surprised? Did the clarity seem to create relief rather than friction?

📚 Source: Keefe Chee, Stage Academy Review (keefechee.com): 'The Coach guides — assertive, strong language, almost no filler words. Short, punchy speech style. Think: a football coach giving instructions to a team. Imagine someone comes to you for motivation but you use the Coach archetype instead — the misalignment erodes trust, especially in a leadership role.'

### Day 5 — The Archetype Sequence — The CTO Signature Move  *[VINH FRAMEWORK]*

Friday. And today I give you the complete move. The sequence that every great CTO, every great keynote speaker, every great leader uses — often without knowing it. Friend opens the door. Motivator creates the urgency. Educator delivers the intelligence. Coach drives the action. Friend closes the loop of trust. This is the architecture of influence. And you already know all four archetypes now — today you wire them together into one fluid pattern. This is what we have been building toward.

Map the Archetype Sequence onto your most important presentation or call happening in the next 5 days. Write out which archetype you will be in for each section.

The Master Sequence: Friend → Motivator → Educator → Coach → Friend. This is the proven flow of a complete, high-impact communication event. It mirrors the neurological progression of a listener: connection → desire → understanding → action → belonging. This sequence, consciously applied to every major call and presentation, is what transforms a senior architect into a CTO communicator.

Take a real presentation you are preparing. Map it explicitly: Section 1 (opening 2 min): Friend. Section 2 (problem statement 3 min): Motivator. Section 3 (solution walkthrough 10 min): Educator. Section 4 (recommendations and ask 5 min): Coach. Section 5 (close and next steps 2 min): Friend. Write one sentence for each section that exemplifies that archetype. ### The Archetype Sequence Quick Card

Open: FRIEND — Warm, personal, melodic, relaxed (60-90 sec)
Shift: MOTIVATOR — Stakes, why it matters, high energy (60-90 sec)
Core: EDUCATOR — Data, structure, BLUF format (main time)
Close: COACH — Clear ask, named owner, specific deadline
Exit: FRIEND — Warmth, openness, invitation to connect

### The Voss Toolkit Quick Card

Mirror: Repeat last 3 words with upward inflection — invite elaboration
Label: 'It seems like...' or 'It sounds like...' — name the emotion
Calibrated Q: 'What would...' or 'How would...' — never Why
Accusation Audit: Name their objections before they voice them
That's Right: Engineer full summary → confirmation of understanding
FM DJ Voice: Slow, low, calm, certain — for any tense moment
