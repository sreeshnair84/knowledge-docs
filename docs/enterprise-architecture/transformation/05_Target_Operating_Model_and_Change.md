## **1. Target Operating Model: Hub, Spokes, and Platform**

We recommend a **federated hub-and-spoke model on a shared platform** . A central AI CoE (hub) owns standards, the platform, scarce expertise, and governance execution; business-unit AI pods (spokes) own use cases and value; the platform team turns repeated needs into products. Pure centralization starves the business of ownership; pure decentralization recreates pilot sprawl. The federation contract below keeps the balance honest.

|**Element**|**Hub (AI CoE)**|**Spokes (BU AI pods)**|**Platform team**|
|---|---|---|---|
|Owns|Strategy execution, standards,<br>governance operations,<br>evaluation service, enablement|Use-case selection, domain<br>data, adoption, benefits delivery|AI platform, golden paths, MCP<br>catalog, SRE for AI|
|Funded by|Central transformation budget|BU P&L; with central<br>co-investment in Horizon 1|Central, with chargeback<br>visibility from Year 2|
|Accountable for|Time-to-production, control<br>effectiveness|Audited business value|Platform reliability, developer<br>experience, unit cost|
|Anti-pattern<br>guarded against|Ivory-tower CoE building demos|Shadow platforms and vendor<br>sprawl|Platform as gatekeeper rather<br>than enabler|

Funding shifts from project-based to **product-based** : persistent teams funded against outcome metrics with quarterly portfolio rebalancing, replacing annual big-bang business cases for the AI portfolio.

## **2. AI Center of Excellence Charter and New Roles**

### **CoE charter (sunset-aware)**

The CoE is chartered for 24-30 months with an explicit ambition to shrink: as spokes mature, delivery capacity transfers to them and the CoE converges on standards, governance, platform, and frontier scanning. A CoE that grows forever has failed.

### **New and evolved roles**

|**Role**|**Mandate**|**Where**|
|---|---|---|
|Chief AI / Transformation<br>Officer|Single accountable executive for the program; chairs Portfolio<br>Board; reports to CEO|Executive team|
|AI Product Manager|Owns an AI product's outcomes, backlog, and benefits case|Spokes (8-15 by Yr 2)|
|AI Engineer / Agent<br>Engineer|Builds RAG apps, agents, and integrations on the paved road|Platform + spokes|
|Evaluation & Quality Lead|Owns golden datasets, eval harness standards, human-review<br>calibration|CoE|
|Knowledge Engineer|Converts expert know-how and content into retrievable,<br>evaluable assets|CoE then spokes|
|AI Risk & Assurance<br>Specialist|Runs tiering, reviews, incident taxonomy; partner to RAI Board|Risk function|

## **4. Change Management Strategy**

### **Workforce principles - stated before rumors state them**

- **Transparency:** leadership communicates honestly which roles change, which shrink, and which grow; silence is the most expensive communication strategy.

- **Reinvestment:** a defined share (recommended 20-30%) of Year 1-2 AI savings funds reskilling and internal mobility before external hiring.

- **Augment-first sequencing:** tools land as assistants before any autonomy; affected teams co-design the workflows that change their jobs.

- **No stealth automation:** process changes with headcount implications go through a defined consultation path; trust, once spent, does not refinance.

### **Adoption engine**

- **Champions network:** 1-2% of workforce as trained champions with real time allocation, not a mailing list.

- **Visible wins cadence:** monthly internal showcases of measured results - adoption follows evidence, not mandates.

- **Friction telemetry:** adoption analytics (active use, task completion, satisfaction) reviewed like product metrics; low adoption is treated as a product defect first, a people problem second.

## **5. Leadership Responsibilities and Incentives**

|**Leader**|**Accountability in the transformation**|
|---|---|
|CEO|Owns the narrative; protects multi-year funding through budget cycles; breaks cross-functional ties|
|CFO|Owns benefits audit discipline and the investment gate process; sponsors the finance lighthouse|
|CIO/CTO|Owns platform delivery and paved-road quality; retires shadow platforms|
|CHRO|Owns skills transformation, workforce principles, and role redesign|
|CRO/GC/CISO|Own two-speed governance that is fast for Tier 3 and rigorous for Tier 1|
|BU Presidents|Own use-case value; AI-attributed outcomes enter their scorecards from Year 1|

*Incentive rule of thumb: if AI outcomes appear in no one's compensation, the transformation is a hobby. Tie a meaningful share of executive variable pay to audited adoption and value metrics from Year 1.*
